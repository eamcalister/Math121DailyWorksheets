    Handout 11.2 Wednesday Week 11 - Combinations of Functions
    

    Objectives

    • Work with combinations of functions.
      🔗
      🔗
    • Explore how relationships between functions give rise to features on the graphs of combinations.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    Another, seemingly simpler, way of creating new functions from old ones is to combine using the operations of arithmetic: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. While these constructions are simple, the way these combinations behave based on how functions relate to one another is important in many situations.
    🔗

    Some observations:
    🔗
    • The product of two functions is zero if and only if one of the factors is zero.
      🔗
      🔗
    • The difference of two functions is zero if and only if the two functions are equal.
      🔗
      🔗
    • The quotient of two functions is zero if and only if when the numerator is zero and the denominator is not zero.
      🔗
      🔗
    • The quotient of two functions is one if and only if the two functions are equal and not zero.
      🔗
      🔗
    • The sign of a product or quotient is determined by the sign of the factors and/or numerator and denominator.
      🔗
      🔗
